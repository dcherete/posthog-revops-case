# Why PostHog

My relationship with PostHog starts in mid-January 2026, but its origin lies in a much older problem.

At Brasil Paralelo we have been running CRM on Insider as our CDP and customer engagement platform since mid-2023, and I can say it is one of the most complete platforms I have ever operated. But no tool is perfect, and over the course of the contract the per-unit price adjustments gradually eroded the cost equation until, in 2026, the pricing reached a point that no longer made sense for our operation. We opened a renegotiation with Insider that dragged on for months, and as time passed the discomfort grew because we were at risk of the contract expiring mid-negotiation and leaving us without a CDP, without a ready alternative, in an operation that sustains 800 thousand active subscribers across 7 different channels.

It was under that pressure that the idea came up, and we started asking whether it made sense to build an open source alternative to Insider, as a way to reduce dependence on a single vendor that had just demonstrated how much it could squeeze pricing when it wanted to. I was put in charge of that investigation and spent weeks searching through GitHub, testing solutions like RudderStack, Dittofeed, Novu, and Listmonk, each one solving a piece of the problem without ever solving the whole thing, until I finally stumbled upon PostHog and fell in love with the solution.

I have always been obsessed with data and user behavior, and that obsession is literally part of why I chose Statistics and then built my entire career around CRM, so seeing what PostHog delivered and the product philosophy behind it was almost a catharsis. From there I went deeper and deeper into the website and the content you produce, especially the full public handbook, which is a display of organizational transparency that very few companies have the courage to publish and that left a deep impression on me, because seeing how you openly document strategy, compensation, and even offboarding processes reveals the kind of culture I have always wanted to find in a workplace.

It was following that thread that I reached the careers page and found a RevOps role that seemed to have been written with someone like me in mind.

My interest here is in PostHog specifically, not in a generic market exit. If it were not for the passion I developed for the company I would probably still be at Brasil Paralelo, because it is a place I genuinely like and whose purpose has truly moved me throughout these years. What is happening now is that the same kind of purpose has started moving me again, only this time through PostHog, and reading about the role I realized I have much of the experience PostHog needs to solve the problems described, and I would very much like to contribute to that.

The fact that the company is product-led (a little different from the culture I am in today), has a strong culture where transparency, autonomy, and ambition are values genuinely practiced, and thrives with a product that makes a real difference in the lives of those who adopt it, makes me believe the team there will teach me a great deal along the way, and in exchange for that I gladly offer my skills and all the knowledge I have built up to here. For all of this, I want to join you.

The open source stack built during that investigation is documented here: [dcherete/bp-crm](https://github.com/dcherete/bp-crm)
