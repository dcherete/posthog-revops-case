The situation I inherited

When I joined Brasil Paralelo, the sales team had 22 reps and a commercial operation that was running on instinct and spreadsheets. There was a compensation model — tiered multipliers, reasonable structure — but no infrastructure to run it reliably at scale. Every month close was a 4-hour scramble to produce a number that should have been automatic.

Over the next two years, I built the data layer that turned that scramble into a system. The team grew to 40 reps. The model evolved from tiered multipliers to OTE. The infrastructure I built supported both without breaking. The four problems below are the specific things I had to solve to get there.

The honest summary

I did not design the compensation model at Brasil Paralelo. I inherited it, operationalized it, automated it, and when leadership wanted to change it I generated the evidence that made the decision possible. I grew with the system from 22 to 40 reps and built the infrastructure that would have allowed it to keep scaling.

PostHog needs someone to do the same thing: take a compensation model that is well-designed but manually operated, build the infrastructure to run it at scale, and be the person who can model the implications of any proposed change before it ships. That is work I have done. The context is different. The operational thinking is the same.
